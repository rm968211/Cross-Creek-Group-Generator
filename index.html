1 The Roster

One player per line in all four boxes. Top to bottom in each box is that player's rank within their tier (1 = top). Each column needs the same number of names.

2 The Events

3 Preferences

A preference is a nudge, not a promise. Heavier weight, harder the solver tries. Two players can share a team in at most one event (twice would be a repeat), and two players from the same letter column can never be teammates.

0

Higher this slider, the harder the solver works to even out each team's total rank (A+B+C+D ranks added up) across every foursome — at 0, rank is ignored entirely. The real question being asked here is how badly do you wanna fuck over Zeke?

? What the Symbols Mean

On the team sheets

  • A5  The player's letter tier and rank within it (1 = top of that tier's list, going down)
  • ❤  A granted preference — these two asked to play together and got it
  • ⚠ + red underline  This player is part of a repeated pairing — check The Card for who and where
  • gold  The player you've selected for a swap
  • green dashed  Legal swap partners for your selection (same letter, same event)

Controls

  • Weight slider (1–10) — how hard the solver tries to grant a wish; 10 is top priority, but no weight can force a repeat
  • Balance by rank slider (0–10) — how hard the solver works to even out team strength using each player's in-tier rank; 0 ignores rank entirely, 10 makes it a top priority (still never forces a repeat)
  • 🔓 / 🔒 lock icon — click a team's padlock to freeze that exact foursome; the next Generate leaves every locked team alone and only reshuffles the rest. Handy once a batch of teams looks right and you just want the solver to keep working on the ones that don't yet
  • Save / Load — one file holds everything: roster, events, preferences, the two-man picks, the roommates, the tee order, and the teams if you've generated them. Save from the Save & Export tab, load from Get Started
  • Copy / Excel / Text / Print — on the Save & Export tab; exports every event exactly as shown, hand-swaps included. The Excel workbook comes out in the classic format: Roomies, Rankings, Pairings (in tee-off order), and Winnings

Hover over almost anything in the app for a quick reminder of what it does.

Repeats are penalized far above any preference — the solver only grants a wish if it can do so without making two players share a foursome twice.

1 The Two-Man Teams

Hand-pick every team — there's no solver here. Click a player in the pool to drop them into the next open slot (or click an open slot first to aim the pick). Click a placed player to send them back to the pool. Pool players shaded red would collide with the group you're filling — they already shared a foursome in the four-man events with someone in it, teammates or not.

🏠 Roommates

Completely optional — this just fills the Roomies sheet in the Excel workbook. Two to a room: click a player in the pool to drop them into the next open bed, or click an open bed first to aim the pick. Click a placed player to send them back. Anyone left in the pool? No worries — their row stays blank on the sheet.

2 Who Leads Off?

There's rarely just one right answer — every group is ranked by how smooth the whole day can be if they tee off first in Round 1. ★ means the day literally can't flow better than with them out front (ties are common). Click a group and the whole day — every round's tee order below — rebuilds around them.
