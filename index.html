3 Preferences

A preference is a nudge, not a promise. Heavier weight, harder the solver tries. Two players can share a team in at most one event (twice would be a repeat), and two players from the same letter column can never be teammates. If one player shows up in several preferences (say Denny wants Fletcher, Doug, and Nate), the solver favors landing all of them in the same foursome over granting each wish in a separate event.

0

Higher this slider, the harder the solver works to even out each team's total rank (A+B+C+D ranks added up) across every foursome — at 0, rank is ignored entirely. The real question being asked here is how badly do you wanna fuck over Zeke?

? What the Symbols Mean

On the team sheets

  • A5  The player's letter tier and rank within it (1 = top of that tier's list, going down)
  • ❤  A granted preference — these two asked to play together and got it
  • ⚠ + red underline  This player is part of a repeated pairing — check The Card for who and where
  • gold  The player you've selected for a swap
  • green dashed  Legal swap partners for your selection (same letter, same event)

Controls

  • Weight slider (1–10) — how hard the solver tries to grant a wish; 10 is top priority, but no weight can force a repeat
  • Balance by rank slider (0–10) — how hard the solver works to even out team strength using each player's in-tier rank; 0 ignores rank entirely, 10 makes it a top priority (still never forces a repeat)
  • 🔓 / 🔒 lock icon — click a team's padlock to freeze that exact foursome; the next Generate leaves every locked team alone and only reshuffles the rest. Handy once a batch of teams looks right and you just want the solver to keep working on the ones that don't yet
  • Save / Load — one file holds everything: roster, events, preferences, and the teams if you've generated them. Load it and you're back exactly where you left off
  • Copy / CSV / Text / Print — exports the sheet exactly as shown, including your hand-swaps

Hover over almost anything in the app for a quick reminder of what it does.

Repeats are penalized far above any preference — the solver only grants a wish if it can do so without making two players share a foursome twice.
