Est. Annually · 28 Players · 7 Foursomes

Cross Creek Pairings

Every team an A · B · C · D — nobody rides the same cart twice

1 The Roster

Each column needs the same number of names. Seven per column makes seven foursomes.

2 The Events

Rename as needed. These are the four-man events — the two-man and the Hangover pick their own teams.

3 Preferences

A preference is a nudge, not a promise. Heavier weight, harder the solver tries. Two players can share a team in at most one event (twice would be a repeat), and two players from the same letter column can never be teammates.

4 Tee It Up

Ready when you are.

Repeats are penalized far above any preference — the solver only grants a wish if it can do so without making two players share a foursome twice.
